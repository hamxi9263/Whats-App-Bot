## SECTION: 6. Pre & Post Procedure Guidelines

### SUBSECTION: 6.1 Pre-Procedure Instructions (7 Days Before)

- Stop blood thinners (aspirin, ibuprofen) — consult your prescribing doctor first
- Avoid alcohol for 7 days before surgery
- Stop smoking for at least 2 weeks before the procedure

- Do not apply any hair products (oils, gels, sprays) on the day of surgery
- Eat a light meal 2 hours before the procedure
- Wear loose, comfortable clothing with a button-front shirt (not pullover)
- Arrange for someone to drive you home post-surgery

- Inform us of all medications and supplements you are taking

### SUBSECTION: 6.2 Post-Procedure Care (First 2 Weeks)

Day Care Instructions

Day 1–3 Keep transplanted area dry. Sleep with head elevated at 45°. Take prescribed
antibiotics and pain relief.
Day 4–7 Gentle saline sprays on transplanted area. First wash with provided medicated
shampoo on Day 4.

Day 7–10 Scabs begin to fall off naturally — do NOT pick or scratch. Regular gentle
washing allowed.
Day 10–14 Most scabs gone. Hair in transplanted area may shed — this is NORMAL (shock
loss phase).

Week 3–6 Shock loss peak. Continue medications. Avoid direct sun exposure.
Month 3–4 New hair growth begins. Thin and light at first.

Month 6–9 Significant growth visible. Texture and thickness improving.
Month 12–18 Final results fully visible. 90%+ density achieved.

### SUBSECTION: 6.3 What to Avoid After Surgery

- Swimming pools, sea, or steam rooms for 4 weeks
- Strenuous exercise or heavy lifting for 2 weeks

- Direct sunlight on scalp for 6 weeks (use a hat)
- Scratching or rubbing the transplanted area
- Hair dye or chemical treatments for 3 months
- Alcohol for 2 weeks post-surgery

### SUBSECTION: 7.3 Frequently Asked Questions

[DATA_TYPE: FAQ]
**Q: Is hair transplant permanent?**

Yes. Transplanted hair follicles are taken from the DHT-resistant donor zone (back and sides of
the scalp). These follicles retain their genetic resistance and continue to grow for a lifetime.
However, the non-transplanted native hair may continue to thin, requiring future sessions or
medications.

[DATA_TYPE: FAQ]
**Q: At what age can I get a hair transplant?**
The minimum recommended age is 25 years. This ensures hair loss has stabilized to a
predictable pattern. There is no upper age limit — many patients in their 60s and 70s have
successful procedures, provided they are in good health.

[DATA_TYPE: FAQ]
**Q: Is the procedure painful?**

The procedure is performed under local anesthesia. Patients experience mild discomfort during
the anesthesia injection phase (approx. 10–15 minutes). After that, the area is numb and most
patients watch movies or sleep during the procedure. Post-operative soreness is managed with
prescribed pain medication.

[DATA_TYPE: FAQ]
**Q: How long until I see results?**

Hair growth follows this timeline: transplanted hair sheds in weeks 2–6 (normal shock loss), new
growth begins at months 3–4, significant density visible at months 6–9, and final result achieved
at 12–18 months.

[DATA_TYPE: FAQ]
**Q: What is the success rate?**
HairRevive Clinic reports a 97% graft survival rate for FUE procedures and 98.2% for DHI.
Success depends on surgeon skill, patient's donor density, and post-operative care compliance.

[DATA_TYPE: FAQ]
**Q: Can women get hair transplants?**

Yes. Women with female pattern baldness, traction alopecia, or scarring alopecia are excellent
candidates. Dr. Ayesha Karim and Dr. Sana Mirza specialize in female hair restoration. The DHI
technique is preferred for women as it does not require complete head shaving.

[DATA_TYPE: FAQ]
**Q: What is the difference between FUE and DHI?**

In FUE, channels are created first, then grafts are implanted. In DHI, a special Choi implanter
pen is used to simultaneously create the channel and implant the graft in one step. DHI offers
higher density and directional control but takes longer and is more expensive.

[DATA_TYPE: FAQ]
**Q: How many sessions will I need?**
Most patients require 1–2 sessions. A single session covers 1,000–5,000+ grafts. Patients with
very extensive baldness (Norwood 6–7) or those desiring maximum density may opt for a
second session 12–18 months after the first.
